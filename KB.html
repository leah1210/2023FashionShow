Karli Banas


I grew up outside of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania and I liked to express myself creatively from a young age. Growing up, I enjoyed drawing and painting in my art classes and always insisted on picking my own outfits for school. Although I have always had a love for fashion, it took me a while to realize that it was something I wanted to do for a career. Fashion is important to me because it helped me to grow my confidence and I hope my designs allow others to do the same. Outside of fashion I enjoy dance, making jewelry, and exploring new places.


The name of my collection is Belladonna. Belladonna is a poisonous plant, and the word translates to “beautiful woman” in Italian. However, small amounts of the belladonna plant are used in different medications and herbal remedies. My collection combines feminine elements like ruffles with punk elements like metal hardware to challenge the way society portrays women. Women who embrace their femininity by enjoying things like makeup and dresses are typically viewed as being “delicate” and “emotional,” while women who have a more punk style with piercings, dyed hair, and distressed clothing are seen as “wild” and “obnoxious”. I wanted my collection to show that there is more than one side to a woman and how she can be feminine, bold, sensitive, confident, kind, and outspoken no matter how she chooses to dress. I took inspiration from female artists like Avril Lavigne, Maggie Lindemann, and Olivia Rodrigo and their girly punk looks.
