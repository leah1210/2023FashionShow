About Our Program


Stevensonâ€™s Fashion Design program develops the individual aesthetic of the designer through art and fashion design courses incorporating theory, technology and professional practice. Students gain an understanding of the fashion industry and history, consumer psychology, materials, and techniques of apparel construction. The coursework focuses primarily on studio work and other discipline-specific classes central to developing a design voice and vision as a fashion design professional.



Highlights from 2022-2023


  • 13 students participated in the fashion study course with a tour of Milan and Florence, Italy during Spring Break 2023
  • Student research presentations from Fashion Illustration and Flat pattern courses presented at the Paul D. Lack Scholarship Showcase
  • Technical Illustration students collaborated with VME Imaging to design clothing for people with disabilities
  • Product Development course designed bags with local accessories designer, Jane O'Leary
  • Student/faculty spring 2023 exhibition Conversations Between, featured designing fabric on a floor loom
  • Fashion Design and Fashion Merchandising presented sustainability in fashion during Stevenson's Earth Week through the lifecycle of denim jeans
  • Fashion students networked with design and merchandising professionals from Under Armour, Ralph Lauren, Lilly Pulitzer, and others in the annual FAD club's Brunch with Bosses event
